BIOGRAPHY

Planning to study science but are feeling unsure about it? We have just the blog for you! If you want to become an innovator, then you must read about great scientists from around the world and draw inspiration from their work!



Nikola Tesla

BORN IN 1856
DEATH IN 1943

Nikola Tesla

Nikola Tesla was a Serbian-American inventor, electrical engineer, mechanical engineer, and futurist best known for his contributions to the design of the modern alternating current electricity supply system.

Marie Curie

BORN IN 1867
DEATH IN 1964

Marie Curie

Marie Curie, née Maria Salomea Skłodowska, (born November 7, 1867, Warsaw, Congress Kingdom of Poland, Russian Empire—died July 4, 1934, near Sallanches, France), Polish-born French physicist, famous for her work on radioactivity and twice a winner of the Nobel Prize.

Isaac Newton

BORN IN 1642
DEATH IN 1727

Isaac Newton

Isaac Newton is best known for having invented the calculus in the mid to late 1660s (most of a decade before Leibniz did so independently, and ultimately more influentially) and for having formulated the theory of universal gravity — the latter in his Principia

Charles Darwin

BORN IN 1809
DEATH IN 1882

Charles Darwin

Charles Robert Darwin FRS FRGS FLS FZS JP was an English naturalist, geologist, and biologist, widely known for contributing to the understanding of evolutionary biology.

Ada Lovelace

BORN IN 1815
DEATH IN 1852

Ada Lovelace

Augusta Ada King, Countess of Lovelace was an English mathematician and writer, chiefly known for her work on Charles Babbage's proposed mechanical general-purpose computer, the Analytical Engine

Rosalind Franklin

BORN IN 1920
DEATH IN 1958

Rosalind Franklin

Rosalind Elsie Franklin was a British chemist and X-ray crystallographer whose work was central to the understanding of the molecular structures of DNA, RNA, viruses, coal, and graphite.
